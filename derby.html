Method. POWER = 90th-pct exit velo, max distance, rate of 400-ft balls. ELEVATE = average launch angle and share of batted balls in the 20–40° window. PARK FIT = each hitter's own spray profile run through Citizens Bank Park's sector-by-sector home-run surface, which we learned empirically from our own batted-ball data — no dimensions were looked up. Phillies get a home-park bump. Ranges are the 10th–90th percentile of a 1,000-sample bootstrap: the fewer batted balls a hitter has, the wider his range.

What this can't see: derby pitching is grooved 65-mph BP, not major-league stuff — and we have no data on stamina or pace, which are real derby skills. Treat these as swing-profile probabilities, not prophecy.
